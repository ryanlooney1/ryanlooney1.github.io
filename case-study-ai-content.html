Overview
The challenge

AI created major new opportunities for content design, but teams needed clearer frameworks, stronger guidance, and practical ways to apply emerging tools responsibly.

My role

I helped define best practices, pioneered system prompt writing, mentored content designers, and supported teams navigating new AI-driven product work.

The outcome

This work helped teams move faster and more confidently, while raising the quality and consistency of AI content decisions across products.

The challenge

As AI capabilities advanced, product teams were moving quickly, but the content discipline around them was still emerging. Teams needed support not just with interface copy, but with deeper questions: how should AI behave, what should it say, how should prompts be structured, and how could content designers contribute strategically to this new space?

Key insight

AI content work is not just about output quality. It requires clear systems thinking around behavior, tone, accuracy, trust, and how language shapes the product itself.

Approach
1. Defined emerging practices

I helped establish how content design could contribute to AI product work, including prompt writing, behavioral guidance, and reusable decision frameworks.

2. Enabled teams directly

I partnered with product teams navigating AI features, helping them make stronger decisions about content structure, model behavior, and user-facing language.

3. Built for scale

I focused on frameworks and principles that could be reused across teams, while also mentoring content designers entering a fast-changing and ambiguous space.
