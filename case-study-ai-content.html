CD Adventures in System Prompting: A Playbook

This is the story of how I became involved in system prompt engineering at Instagram, what I learned, what I proved, and what every CD should know about translating craft expertise into better AI outputs.

When Instagram's GenAI team began building LLM-powered ad CTAs, content design wasn't part of the process. I changed that, and built this playbook so that you can jump in and scale your impact, too.

System prompts are content design

When Instagram's GenAI team started building AI-generated ad CTAs, content design wasn't in the room. Based on my work on CD-curated ad CTAs, I embedded myself into the process, wrote the system prompt from a CD lens, and proved that treating prompt instructions as a content deliverable could multiply impact at scale.

Approach & Process
Step 01
Align & Access

Got leadership buy-in to embed CD into the system prompt workflow, met with key POCs, and learned system prompting from Content Engineers across Meta.

→
Step 02
Audit & Rewrite

Identified patterns and gaps in the existing prompt. Rewrote it section by section, bringing CD expertise: the ingredients of an effective CTA, what to prioritize, and what to avoid to de-risk outputs.

→
Step 03
Test & Iterate

Partnered with Eng to test improvements in a sandbox. Developed quality evaluation criteria with PM, ran quality parties, and iterated until results were consistently stronger than baseline CTAs.

→
Step 04
Ship & Measure

Aligned on public test, kicked off, and measured results: 3%+ CTR on LLM-powered CTAs, with new rounds of testing ongoing.

Pioneering system prompting for CDs at Meta

I documented the process and built a replicable playbook for CDs leading system prompt writing at Meta. Published it org-wide, hosted weekly office hours, and influenced Instagram leadership to establish system prompt writing as a new CD deliverable.

Challenge

By the time Instagram's GenAI teams were building LLM-powered features, I had already spent time on Bank of America's Erica AI team, working at the intersection of language and intelligent systems. Working on Erica gave me a unique vantage point. The quality of writing inside an AI, the instructions shaping every output, directly determines the quality of what users experience at scale.

When I saw that Instagram's LLM-powered ad CTAs were being built without content design input, I recognized the opportunity immediately. The words reaching users at scale were being shaped entirely by engineers. Content design had a role to play, and nobody had claimed it yet. I had the curiosity and background to change that.
