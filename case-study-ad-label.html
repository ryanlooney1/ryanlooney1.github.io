Simplifying Meta's global ad label

Changing "Sponsored" to "Ad" across Meta apps to improve clarity, reduce visual clutter, and unlock meaningful revenue.

After
Challenge

"Sponsored" had appeared on Meta ads since 2006. Over time it became infrastructure — never revisited, never optimized, assumed correct. I identified it as an untapped opportunity: a single high-frequency label that, if improved, could reduce visual clutter, improve comprehension, and unlock design and monetization possibilities across every Meta surface.

The problem was that changing it was genuinely risky. Meta had existing agreements with the FTC that referenced the label. If we changed it on Instagram, we'd need to change it everywhere — Facebook, Messenger, Threads, every app. Legal and policy teams had real concerns. Leadership wasn't convinced the cross-functional effort was worth the organizational cost.

Approach
1. Made the business case

I positioned the work as both a clarity win and a monetization opportunity — not a copy preference. That reframe was what got leadership to take it seriously.

2. Navigated legal and policy

I worked through FTC-related concerns with legal and policy teams, helping find a path that satisfied compliance requirements and gave the project room to move.

3. Led global testing and alignment

I pushed to test the change at scale and coordinated with translation teams and XFN partners across apps on rationale, sequencing, and timing.

Impact
  • Initial tests showed significant increases in dwell time and click rates, major consideration signals
  • Launched in early 2026 across Meta's apps
  • Unlocked millions in revenue opportunities with new placements thanks to the shorter, simpler labels

Key insight

This was not just a copy change. A single-word decision touched FTC compliance, cross-app consistency, translation pipelines, monetization strategy, and the trust of billions of users.
