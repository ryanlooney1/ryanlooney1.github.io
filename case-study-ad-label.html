Challenge

"Sponsored" had appeared on Meta ads since 2006. Over time it became inherited infrastructure that was unquestioned and overlooked. I identified it as an untapped opportunity: a single high-frequency label that, if improved, could reduce visual clutter, improve comprehension, and unlock design and monetization possibilities across every Meta surface.

The problem was that changing it was genuinely risky. Meta had existing agreements with the FTC that referenced the label. If we changed it on Instagram, we'd also need to change it on Facebook, Messenger, Threads, WhatsApp -- everywhere. Legal and policy teams had real concerns. Leadership wasn't convinced the cross-functional effort was worth the organizational cost.

"Ryan quickly became the go-to expert on the label change, guiding both Facebook and WhatsApp content design teams and ensuring we had a unified approach."
Manager, Facebook Design
Approach
1. Made the business case

I positioned this not as a copy preference, but as a win for simplicity and clarity that represented a massive revenue opportunity (win-win). That reframe was what got leadership to take it seriously.

2. Navigated legal and policy

I worked through FTC-related concerns with legal and policy teams, helping find a path that satisfied compliance requirements and gave the project room to move.

3. Led global testing and alignment

I pushed to test the change at scale and coordinated with translation teams and XFN partners across apps on rationale, sequencing, and timing.

Impact
  • Initial tests showed significant increases in dwell time and click rates, major consideration signals
  • Launched in early 2026 across Meta's apps
  • Unlocked millions in revenue opportunities with new placements thanks to the shorter, simpler labels

Key insight

This was not just a copy change. A single-word decision touched FTC compliance, cross-app consistency, translation pipelines, monetization strategy, and the trust of billions of users.
